Foods from around the World.

A website with foods from the countries of where different refugee communities in Colorado used to live. The foods below are some of the foods that you can see if you click on a specific button at the top.

Caakiri

Caakiri is a dessert from the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It is a form of couscous, for dessert! The photo above shows couscous, which is the pasta it is made out of but not it.

Dabo Kolo

Dabo Kolo is a snack from the Democratic Republic of the Congo that is known for being both crispy and it has spice! The photo above has the ingredients, not it itself.

Burmese dried fish with egg curry

This is a delicious dish from Myanmar, with not too expensive ingredients, and is useful for large crowds. The photo above has egg curry, but it doesn't have dried fish and isn't necessarily from Mayanmar.

Mohinga

Mohinga is a dish from Mayanmar with rice noodles with soup and fried pieces.

Kabuli Pulao

Kabuli Pulao is a dish that is very popular in Afghanistan. It has few ingredients, and tastes great!

Mantu

Mantu is a dish from Afghanistan. Mantu is a pastry filled with meat, that is either boiled or steamed.

Sheer Khurma

Sheer Khurma is a dish from Afghanistan. It is sweet, and has vermicelli, milk and fruits.

Burmese Biryani

This dish from Mayanmar is amazing. It even has different some components! This picture above is of biryani but not necessarily Burmese biryani.

Liboke

This popular food from the Democratic Republic of the Congo includes both tomatoes and fish! The photo on the left has liboke, but it is not neccessarily from the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

Why is it important to learn about foods in different places?

I was inspired to make this website by the book Everything Sad is Untrue by Daniel Nayeri. In the book, Daniel sometimes feels out of place with what he eats. When his friend Kyle came over to his house, he refused to eat what they had just because he hadn't ever had it before. This concept comes up many times throughout the book. This is probably still coming up today, and the sooner we can recognize foods from different places, the more open to them we can be. This could make a small difference in how welcomed refugees who are coming to Colorado feel.

This website will focus on recipies from:

Democratic Republic of the Congo

Democratic Republic of the Congo is a country in Africa. That was the home of many refugees who have come to Colorado.

Myanmar

Mayanmar is a country near China. It was the home of many refugees who now live in Colorado. The photo says Burma because that used to be the name of the country. Learn more about it when you click the option at the top "Foods from Myanmar".

Afghanistan

Afghanistan is also a country near China. Many people from Afghanistan have come to Colorado for a safe place to live.
